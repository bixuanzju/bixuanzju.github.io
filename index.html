About Me

I’m now on the job market for a full-time industrial research and/or development job starting Spring 2019. If you are interested, drop me a line! I’m especially interested in language design related projects (using Haskell, OCaml or whatever statically-typed languages available).

I am a PhD candidate at HKU PL Group advised by Dr. Bruno C. d. S. Oliveira. My research interests are centered around programming languages, type systems and formal verification.

In the past, I have worked on a JVM implementation of System F with support for full tail-call elimination. See our paper for more. I have worked on the design of a dependently-typed calculus featuring general recursion and decidable type-checking. See our paper for more. I have also worked on the design of a gradual type system with support for implicit higher-rank polymorphism. Think of it as Haskell with gradual typing. See our paper for more.

Currently I am working on the design of a type system with disjoint intersection types, which has great potential to serve as a foundation for powerful, flexible and yet type-safe and easy to reason OO languages. See this paper for the theoretical foundation and that for the first design of typed first-class traits. My PhD thesis draft contains a more in-depth and consistent treatment of disjoint intersection types
